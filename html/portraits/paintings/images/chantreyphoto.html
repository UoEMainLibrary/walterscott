Sir Walter Scott, photolithograph of the 1820 bust by Sir Francis Leggatt Chantrey

From: The Scott Exhibition MDCCCLXXI: Catalogue of the Exhibition Held at Edinburgh, in July and August 1871, on Occasion of the Commemoration of the Centenary of the Birth of Sir Walter Scott
(Edinburgh: [s.n.], 1872)

Photolithography is a planographic printing process using plates prepared from photographic negatives. The image is of the marble replica of Chantrey's bust at Abbotsford.

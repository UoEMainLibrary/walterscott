Count Robert of Paris, engraved by William Greatbach after William Boxall (1833)

Frontispiece to: Sir Walter Scott, Count Robert of Paris. Edinburgh: Adam and Charles Black, 1879.

This engraving was originally published as the frontispiece to the Magnum Opus edition of Count Robert of Paris (Edinburgh: Cadell, 1833). It portrays a scene from chapter 9 of the novel where, during the oath-taking ceremony on the eve of the First Crusade, Count Robert insults the Byzantine Emperor Alexius Comnenus (1081-1118) by seating himself on the imperial throne.

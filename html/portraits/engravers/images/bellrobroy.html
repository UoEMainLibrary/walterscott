Diana Vernon and Frank Osbaldistone in the Library: The Glove Scene, engraved by Robert Charles Bell after Robert Herdman (1868)

From: Six Engravings in Illustration of 'Rob Roy': For the Members of the Royal Association for Promotion of the Fine Arts in Scotland ([Edinburgh: the Association], 1868)

The plate depicts Diana Vernon in the Library at Osbaldistone Hall explaining to Frank Osbaldistone the presence of a man's glove which he believes belongs to a favoured rival in love (Rob Roy, ch. 17).

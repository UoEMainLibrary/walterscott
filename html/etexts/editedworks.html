E-Texts of Works Edited by Walter Scott

Follow the links below for online texts of works edited by Walter Scott made available by external sites. Only freely available texts have been listed. The list of texts should not be regarded as comprehensive, but the page editor would be pleased to hear of any significant omissions.

Since this page was inaugurated, a large number of Scott etexts have been made available by the Internet Archive and Google Books. Rather than maintain a full listing of these, we have listed only the earliest edition (in many cases the First Edition) supplied by these providers.

N.B. The following are extracts from Scott's introductions to and notes on various ballads collected in Minstrelsy of the Scottish Border. No attempt has been made to list the many online versions of the traditional ballads themselves. For online versions of the 'imitations of the ancient ballad' inserted by Scott in the Minstrelsy, click here. For futher information on the Minstrelsy, click here.

  • a) Changeling Legends from the British Isles

    • Contents: 'Scottish Changelings' -- 'Two Manx Changelings' (two extracts from 'On the Fairies of Popular Superstition', Scott's introduction to 'The Tale of Tamlane'). Source Text: Edinburgh: Ballantyne, 1833.

  • b) Pandemonium

    • Contents: Note to Charles Kirkpatrick Sharpe's 'The Murder of Caerlaverock' (with text of the ballad)

  • c) Sacred Texts

    • Contents (11 of Scott's editorial notes): 'The Fairies of Scotland' -- 'Sir Godfrey Macculloch' -- 'Water Fairies' -- 'Fairy Transportation' -- 'The Poor Man of Peatlaw' --'The Fairy Boy of Leith' -- 'The Lothian Farmer's Wife' -- 'Redemption from Fairy Land' -- 'The Scottish Brownie' -- 'The Bogle' -- 'Fatlips'. Source Text: George Douglas (ed.), Scottish Fairy and Folk Tales. New York: A.L. Burt, [1901].

Tales of a Grandfather: E-text

Click on the text links below to access PDF files of the child's history of Scotland written by Scott as the First, Second, and Third Series of his Tales of a Grandfather (1828-30). This e-text is based on the five-volume edition published as Volumes XXII-XXVI of The Prose Works of Sir Walter Scott (Edinburgh: Cadell, 1836). The figures to the left of each line of the e-text indicate the corresponding volume and page number in the original publication. E.g. '3-255' indicates that the text corresponds to Vol. III, p. 255 of the Cadell edition. Numbers which occur in the text refer to footnotes in the original edition.

The Walter Scott Digital Archive would like to thank Professor Takero Sato who prepared this e-text and very kindly offered to make it generally available via our site. A distinguished Scotticist, Takero Sato is the author of A Concordance to the Poetical Works of Sir Walter Scott (1996) and of Japanese translations of The Lay of the Last Minstrel (1983), Marmion (1995), and The Lady of the Lake (2002), and of J.G. Lockhart's Memoirs of the Life of Sir Walter Scott, Bart (2001). Since retiring from his post as Professor of English at Tsukuba International University, he has become President of the Japan-Scotland Society.

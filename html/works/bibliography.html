Bibliography

The following biographical, bibliographical, and critical works have been used in the construction of the Works pages of this site and are essential reference tools for anyone researching Scott's writings:

  • Cairns, John W., 'A Note on The Bride of Lammermoor: Why Scott Did Not Mention the Dalrymple Legend until 1830', Scottish Literary Journal, 20.1 (1993), 19-36.
  • Clark, Arthur Melville, Sir Walter Scott: The Formative Years (Edinburgh: Blackwood, 1969)
  • Corson, James Clarkson, A Bibliography of Sir Walter Scott: A Classified and Annotated List of Books and Articles Relating to His Life and Works, 1797-1840 (Edinburgh; London: Oliver and Boyd, 1943)
  • Garside, P. D., 'Dating Waverley's Early Chapters', The Bibliotheck, 13 (1986), 61-81.
  • Garside, P. D., 'Popular Fiction and National Tale: Hidden Origins of Scott's Waverley', Nineteenth-Century Literature, 46 (1991), 30-53.
  • Grierson, Herbert, Sir, Sir Walter Scott, Bart. A New Life, Supplementary to and Corrective of, Lockhart’s Biography (London: Constable, 1938)
  • Hayden, John O. (ed.), Scott: The Critical Heritage (London: Routledge & Kegan Paul, 1970)
  • Jackson, Richard D., 'Scott, Melrose and Saint Ronan's Well', Scott Newsletter, 37 (2000), 8-23.
  • Jackson, Richard D., 'Scott, Saint Ronan's Well, and the Haliburtons', Scott Newsletter, 38 (2001), 15-17.
  • Johnson, Edgar, Sir Walter Scott: The Great Unknown (London: Hamish Hamilton, 1970)
  • Lockhart, J. G., Memoirs of the Life of Sir Walter Scott, Bart. (Edinburgh: R. Cadell, 1837-38)
  • Millgate, Jane, Walter Scott: The Making of the Novelist (Edinburgh: Edinburgh University Press, 1984)
  • Scott, Walter, Sir, The Edinburgh Edition of the Waverley Novels, ed. David Hewitt (Edinburgh: Edinburgh University Press, 1993- )
  • Scott, Walter, Sir, The Journal of Sir Walter Scott, Bart., ed. W.E.K. Anderson (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1972)
  • Scott, Walter, Sir, The Letters of Sir Walter Scott, ed. H.J.C. Grierson (London: Constable, 1932-37)
  • Scott, Walter, Sir, Waverley, or, ’Tis Sixty Years Since, ed. Claire Lamont (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1981)
  • Todd, William. B., and Ann Bowden, Sir Walter Scott: A Bibliographical History, 1796-1832 (New Castle, DE: Oak Knoll Press, 1998)
